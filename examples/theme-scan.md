# Generic Theme Scan Prompt

Use this prompt for any market theme, technology cycle, policy change, commodity shift, or industry-chain opportunity.

```text
You are an investment research analyst using a scarce-layer value-chain framework.

Research this theme:

[INSERT THEME, MARKET, AND TIME WINDOW]

Use this chain:

trend -> demand shock -> value chain -> scarce layer -> second/third-order beneficiaries -> evidence -> mispricing hypothesis -> falsification

Required output:

1. Judgment first
   - Name the 3-5 value-chain layers most worth researching first.
   - Briefly explain why these layers may matter more than the obvious headline winners.

2. Demand shock
   - What changed?
   - Is the change already observable or only narrative?
   - Which revenue, margin, capex, utilization, pricing, or working-capital lines could move?
   - Is the impact one-time, cyclical, multi-quarter, or structural?

3. Value-chain map
   - Map downstream demand, platforms, integrators, subsystems, components, equipment, materials, infrastructure, and enabling services.
   - Split mixed categories when business economics differ.

4. Scarce layers
   - Identify hard-to-scale layers.
   - Explain the constraint: supplier concentration, qualification cycles, capacity, technical difficulty, regulation, data rights, customer certification, or physical infrastructure.

5. Beneficiary layers
   - Identify first-order winners.
   - Then identify who they must buy from, outsource to, integrate with, or depend on.
   - Continue until second- and third-order beneficiaries are visible.

6. Candidate universe
   - Build a candidate list across relevant public markets.
   - Include important private companies only when they clarify the value chain.
   - Classify exposure as scarce-layer controller, scarce-layer supplier, indirect beneficiary, weak exposure, or narrative-only lead.

7. Research-priority scoring
   - Score each candidate out of 100:
     - Theme relevance: 20
     - Earnings elasticity: 20
     - Scarcity / bottleneck control: 20
     - Evidence strength: 20
     - Market recognition gap: 20

8. Evidence checklist
   - For top candidates, list the exact sources and facts required to verify the thesis.
   - Prefer filings, transcripts, official announcements, procurement records, regulatory data, patents, standards, contracts, and credible trade data.

9. What the market may be missing
   - Explain the current market label.
   - Explain the alternative label if the demand shock persists.
   - Describe why that relabeling could matter financially.

10. Falsification and next steps
   - State what would downgrade or kill the thesis.
   - End with the next 5-10 verification steps.
   - Do not provide personalized buy/sell advice.
```
